Amherst Uprising Goals


Updated Friday, November 20th

Cultural competency: necessitate that fafculty, staff, current and incoming students participate in training, programming and discussions on cultural competency
  • •Implement mandatory training for cultural competency for all faculty and staff, athletes (as part of their LEADS program), first-year students (as part of their first-year seminar and orientation), Resident Counselors, and all leaders of student organizations (in order to receive funding from the Association of Amherst Students)
  • Treat issues of racial misconduct with similar urgency to issues of academic misconduct (i.e. plagiarism) and insist that those who violate student codes of conduct be required to attend cultural competency training


Academic Policy: ensure that the academic environment is accessible and respectful of students’ identities so that they may flourish
  • Revise the Honor Code so that it reflects the institution’s commitment to prevent behaviors that cause racial injury in an inclusive, restorative way for all parties involved
  • Revise the Honor Code so that it reflects a zero-tolerance policy for hate speech
  • Allow students to request tutors before being deemed “as failing” in any course
  • Create opportunities for all student organizations to have faculty advisors
  • Create a Latin American, Caribbean, and Latino Studies major
  • Expand and reform the Asian Languages and Civilizations Studies major


Prospective Students: extend resources to prospective students of diverse backgrounds and ensure their continued support
  • Make transparent the use of criminal record information during the application process
  • Ensure that the varsity recruitment process reflects and retains the intersectional diversity of the campus
  • Extend resources to undocumented, Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals, and international students


Hiring and Retention: increase diversity and ensure continued support of Amherst faculty and staff during and after the hiring process
  • Commit to increasing the number of staff and administrators of color in the Counseling Center, Office of Financial Aid, Office of Alumni and Parent Programs, Health Center, Career Center, and Writing Center, and faculty of color within all academic departments
  • Hire more faculty of color on tenure track lines within all departments
  • Create structures that incentivize faculty members to recruit research assistant that are Pell-Grant eligible


Student Resources: increase the visibility of student resources to support and establish an inclusive community
  • Diversify art displayed in the Mead and elsewhere on campus to reflect the diversity of the Amherst College community
  • Require that Peer Advocates of Sexual Respect and Student Health Educators reflect the diversity of the student body
  • Increase the visibility of resources on campus for mixed status, Deferred Action for Child Arrivals, and undocumented students
  • Increase the visibility of cultural affinity club spaces
  • Create a theme house for Latinx students, as per La Causa’s request
  • Implement and support existing mentor programs for students of varying identities


Funding: improve the funding process so that it supports diversity on campus
  • Increase the budget allocated to the affinity groups and theme houses
  • Improve the reimbursement process to take into account students’ varying socioeconomic statuses
  • Improve the funding processes for clubs, resource centers and staff


Alumni and Outreach: reach out to current and former members of the Amherst community to improve the quality of alumni-student relations, and involve them in Amherst Uprising
  • Insist that the Office of Alumni and Parent Programs support and fund the affinity groups in planning alumni weekends and reunion events


Mental Health: prioritize the mental health of the student body through hiring diverse staff across identity and practice metrics, as well as updating current policies
  • Clarify channels of support and communication for students dealing with mental health issues
  • Implement a follow-up system that handles attendance inconsistencies and ensures at-risk students have proper support
  • Hire more staff for the Counselling Center, specifically psychiatrics
  • Diversify the staff at the Counselling Center to better serve the student body
This list is a summary of the longer list of goals that the student body created on the evening of Saturday, November 14th. This list will be available when committees reconvene after Thanksgiving break.
